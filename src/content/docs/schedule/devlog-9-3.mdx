# DevLog 9 — Final Project Design
## Structura: High-Fidelity Designs

### The Designs

This week I worked on creating high-fidelity designs in Canva for Structura. I started with a moodboard of websites and tools that inspired the visual direction, then moved into designing the individual structure pages and a style guide.

**[View Designs](https://canva.link/pcsezqs8twcoscm)**

I started the moodboard by pulling references from developer tools, coding environments, and educational interfaces that felt dark, minimal, and technical. I wanted Structura to feel like something a programmer would actually use — not a generic educational app with bright colors and clip art.

### Style Guide

For the color scheme I went with a near-black background `#0D0D0D`, a primary green `#00F5A0`, and a secondary purple `#6C63FF`. The green reads as "active" and "alive" which felt right for nodes and animations — like something is actually running. The purple is used for secondary actions and hover states.

For typography I used **JetBrains Mono** as the main font. It's a real developer font and it fits the theme of the project — learning how code actually works under the hood. It also makes the code panel feel natural since the same font shows up in the story column and in the algorithm.

### Structure Page Iterations

**Linked List — Iteration 1:** The story column and sandbox panel had equal visual weight side by side. It felt balanced but the sandbox got lost — your eye didn't know where to go first.

**Linked List — Iteration 2:** I shifted more visual space to the sandbox panel and made the story column narrower and scrollable. This felt better because the interaction is the point, not the reading. The story is the guide, not the focus.

### About Page

I designed one iteration of the About page. It introduces what Structura is and why the critical framing matters — who built these structures, for whom, and what assumptions got baked in. I kept it minimal with mostly text. I wanted it to feel like a short manifesto rather than a project description.

### Reflection

Structura's goal is to teach data structures in a way that actually sticks — through animation and direct manipulation, not memorization. But it also asks a harder question: these structures were designed by specific people with specific assumptions. Who gets left out of that? I want users to walk away thinking about that even if they can't fully answer it yet.

Doing the designs after already building the prototype was actually useful. I already knew what the three-panel layout needed to do so I wasn't guessing — I was just figuring out how to make it look right. The main thing I learned is that the sandbox needs to breathe. It can't be squeezed into a corner. The interaction has to be the center of the page.
