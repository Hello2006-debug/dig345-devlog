### What I'm Making

**Structura** is an interactive web experience that teaches fundamental data structures — linked lists, stacks, queues, trees, and graphs — through visually stunning animation and direct manipulation. Rather than reading static definitions, users interact with living visualizations: pushing nodes onto a stack, watching a tree grow as they insert values, or following a pointer as it traverses a linked list. Structura is both a genuine learning tool and a piece of critical software — one that asks: who designed these structures, for what kinds of data, and whose problems were they built to solve?

### Reflection

The part of this project I'm most excited about is the critical angle — not just teaching data structures, but asking *who built them and for whom*. Most tools like VisuAlgo treat these as neutral, objective knowledge. I want Structura to make learners pause and think about that.

My biggest unknowns right now are technical: I'm not sure yet how to smoothly animate pointer redraws in D3.js when nodes are inserted or deleted, and I don't know whether Framer Motion and D3 will conflict inside React's rendering cycle. The onboarding experience is also unresolved — I want the sandbox to feel intuitive without needing a tutorial, but I haven't figured out how to do that yet.

The first thing I plan to build is a working linked list visualization as a proof of concept, since that's the simplest structure and will tell me quickly whether the core animation approach is feasible.
